One switch flips. Scroll, and that single transistor becomes a chip, a device, a network, a planet of data.

↓

01 — THE CHIP

Billions of switches, one wafer.

The camera pulls back from a single gate to the die: copper traces route between logic blocks, signals racing along the interconnect at a few hundred picoseconds a hop. What looked like a lone switch is a city of them, etched at the nanometre.

02 — THE DEVICE

The die, taken apart.

The package lifts free and the board explodes into layers — silicon, substrate, memory, the power stage and the radio. Each part floats out, labelled, then settles back into the stack that fits in your hand.

03 — THE NETWORK

Every device is a node.

Pull back far enough and the device is one point of light among millions. Links ignite between nodes as packets find their route — an instanced mesh of the whole graph, assembling itself as you scroll outward.

04 — THE DATA

It all resolves to numbers.

      ABOUT THIS BUILD

      A telescope for
      technology, in the browser.

      SUBSTRATE is a scroll-driven Three.js experience: a single continuous timeline where scroll position is a zoom level. The camera dollies across roughly nine orders of magnitude — from a transistor gate to a planet-scale network — and the scene assembles itself at every scale. No downloaded models: the chip, the exploded device and the node graph are all procedural geometry and shaders.
